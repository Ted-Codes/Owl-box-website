About Me

I'm Ted—a high school student, Eagle Scout, amateur carpenter, and programmer with a passion for combining engineering, technology, and conservation. This project began in my woodworking shop, where I discovered a love for building things with purpose. When it came time to complete my Eagle Scout project, I designed and built a nesting box for local owls. My original vision was to install a camera inside and livestream the nesting process so anyone could experience these incredible birds up close. While setting up the livestream has taken longer than expected, it inspired a new idea. Rather than waiting for a single owl box to come online, I built a platform that can analyze *any* publicly available owl box livestream. Using computer vision and AI, it automatically observes activity and transforms hours of video into meaningful data about owl behavior, occupancy, and environmental conditions. My goal extends beyond collecting data. I plan to use this information to create a hands-on educational curriculum for local schools, helping students explore wildlife conservation, ecology, engineering, and computer science through real-world data from nesting owls. What started as a woodworking project has grown into a platform that connects craftsmanship, technology, and environmental education—making wildlife research more accessible while inspiring the next generation of engineers and conservationists.
